■ Origin

      
Why We Built This

      

        Every AI prompt silently consumes electricity, emits CO₂, and evaporates cooling
        water — yet every chat interface shows you exactly none of that.
        The AI Death Clock exists to make the invisible tangible.
      

      

        

          🌫️
          
The Cost Is Hidden

          

            AI inference servers run 24 / 7, globally, at enormous scale.
            The convenience of an instant answer obscures a very real chain of
            resource consumption that most users never see.
          

        

        

          📈
          
Growth Is Exponential

          

            Global AI token consumption has grown by orders of magnitude since
            2020 and is projected to keep accelerating.  Training gets the
            headlines, but inference — the constant, every-second
            generation of tokens — is where most ongoing energy goes.
          

        

        

          🔢
          
Numbers Need Context

          

            “65 quadrillion tokens” means nothing to most people.
            Translating it into kWh spent, CO₂ emitted, and trees required to
            offset it makes the scale viscerally real.
          

        

      

    

  


  
  

    

      
■ Methodology

      
How It Works

      

        We estimate cumulative global token consumption from public data
        (OpenAI usage disclosures, Epoch AI compute trends, AI Index),
        anchor it to a known point in time, and extrapolate forward at the
        current estimated global inference rate of 100 million
        tokens per second.
      

      

        Environmental equivalents are derived from published per-token energy
        figures and IEA / Microsoft sustainability data (see Sources below).
        The milestones translate raw token counts into memorable environmental
        thresholds — not to predict exact doom, but to give scale to what
        these numbers mean.
      

      

        

          
01

          

            Anchor point — a verified token-count estimate is
            fixed to a specific date and time.
          

        

        

          
02

          

            Live extrapolation — tokens tick forward in
            real-time using the current estimated global inference rate.
          

        

        

          
03

          

            Environmental conversion — token count is
            multiplied by per-token energy, CO₂, and water factors from
            peer-reviewed sources.
          

        

        

          
04

          

            Milestone triggers — each environmental threshold
            is crossed once and only once; the card lights up and stays lit.

■ Take Action

      
What You Can Do

      

        Awareness is the first step — but action matters more.  Check the
        
        for the Tips section and the
        Personal Footprint Calculator to see your own impact
        and how to reduce it.
      

      

        

          ✏️
          Write tighter prompts
          
Fewer tokens in means fewer tokens out. Clear, specific questions outperform vague essays every time.

        

        

          🤏
          Use the right model
          
Smaller models handle most everyday tasks at a fraction of the compute cost of frontier models.

        

        

          🔁

Re-reading the same content, regenerating the same boilerplate, or spinning up duplicate contexts all cost tokens unnecessarily.

Share this page.  The more people understand the cost, the more pressure builds on AI providers to improve efficiency.
